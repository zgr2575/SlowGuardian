SlowGuardian v9 - Your Privacy and Security First

Last Updated: January 15, 2025

Section 1: Definitions

In this privacy policy, "We" refers to "SlowGuardian". "Personally-identifiable information" (PII) refers to information that can be used to identify a person such as IP addresses. "Official SlowGuardian deployments" refer to the SlowGuardian application running on the official server. "SlowGuardian deployment operator" refers to the person(s) running the SlowGuardian application. "ToS" refers to Terms of Service.

Please redirect any general inquiries to our official community on GitHub.

Section 2: Summary

  • By using SlowGuardian, you agree you understand and acknowledge this privacy policy and its clauses.
  • SlowGuardian will not be held liable in any situation that may occur as a result of using or hosting the SlowGuardian application on ANY SlowGuardian deployment.
  • The safest way to use SlowGuardian is by using its official deployments or by self-hosting.

Section 2.1: Summary - Official SlowGuardian Deployment

Privacy First: Official SlowGuardian deployments are run with privacy in mind. We do not intentionally collect and retain any PII such as IP addresses used to access the official SlowGuardian deployment.

Section 2.2: Summary - Unofficial SlowGuardian Deployments

  • SlowGuardian developers are committed to ensuring SlowGuardian remains as a secure application. However, we cannot guarantee the same level of security or privacy of unofficial SlowGuardian deployments.
  • The type of information collected on community deployments is completely dependent on who and where the deployment is being hosted.
  • Generally, we recommend SlowGuardian deployment operators to collect as minimal information as possible.
  • We cannot control unofficial SlowGuardian deployments.
  • We cannot verify the authenticity of the code in unofficial SlowGuardian deployments. This is an extremely rare situation, but we will not be held responsible if your data such as an account becomes compromised as a result of malicious code in an unofficial SlowGuardian deployment.

Section 3: Acceptable Use

Important Guidelines:

  • Do not use any SlowGuardian deployment to do illegal activity.
  • If you are under 18, do not use any SlowGuardian deployment to access any adult sites.
  • Respect the terms of service of websites you visit through SlowGuardian.
  • Do not attempt to circumvent network security measures beyond intended proxy functionality.

Section 5: The Information We Collect

Section 5.1: The Official SlowGuardian Deployment

Minimal Data Collection: Information that may be unintentionally collected as part of using SlowGuardian's official deployment may include basic information such as your IP address, web traffic when using SlowGuardian, etc. This information is temporary and is kept until the server is rebooted.

  • We do not purposefully collect anyone's information. We try to limit the data collected by the official SlowGuardian deployment.
  • We do not, will not, and will never knowingly collect the information of minors under the age of 13 (or the minimum age required by laws in your country).
  • User preferences and settings are stored locally in your browser.

Section 5.2: Unofficial SlowGuardian Deployments

  • We can't give a definitive answer on what information is collected when you use an unofficial SlowGuardian deployment, only the operator of the SlowGuardian deployment you're using can.
  • We urge operators to collect and store as minimal information as possible, but we can't control what they do.

Section 6: Data Control & Security

  • We do not have the power to control what unofficial SlowGuardian deployment operators do with their deployment.
  • We protect information stored on the official SlowGuardian deployment by limiting access and implementing security best practices.
  • We cannot check every deployment for malicious code. If any data such as your account gets compromised after you access it on an unofficial SlowGuardian deployment, we will not be held liable.
  • All proxy traffic is encrypted and secured using industry-standard protocols.

Section 7: How We Use Information

  • Your IP address is used to allow you to use the SlowGuardian service. This is how it works for every website you visit, not just SlowGuardian. The difference is whether or not this data is intentionally collected and stored.
  • Info about your web traffic while using SlowGuardian is needed to allow you to access the service you wish to access.
  • SlowGuardian will NEVER sell your info to third-parties. We may display ads to support the service, but personal data is never shared.
  • The official SlowGuardian server operator may respond to legal requests and assist law enforcement officials in legal investigations if ever needed. We can't guarantee we'll always have information to hand out as the information that may be collected is automatically destroyed on reboot, but if we do still have information relevant to the investigation, we may provide law enforcement officials with it.
